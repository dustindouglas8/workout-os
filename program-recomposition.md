# Program: Recomposition v1

## Design Principles

This program is built on five research-backed decisions. Every session structure follows from them.

1. **More first sets per week, not more sets per session.** A first working set delivers ~100% of its hypertrophy stimulus. Each subsequent set in the same session delivers ~25–30% less stimulus but equal fatigue. This program distributes first sets across 5 days rather than stacking 6–8 sets per muscle per session.

2. **Antagonist supersets as the primary time lever.** Pairing push + pull (or squat + hinge) eliminates inter-set rest without reducing quality. Research (Robbins et al. 2010) shows antagonist pre-fatigue does not reduce agonist performance — it may improve it via reciprocal inhibition. Result: 40-minute sessions with the stimulus of 60-minute straight-set sessions.

3. **Proximity to failure controls recovery cost, not intensity.** 2 RIR (two reps in reserve) keeps you in the effective rep zone — the last 5 reps before failure drive hypertrophy — without the 4-day recovery debt of training to failure. Intensity stays constant across all three phases. Only volume changes.

4. **Zone 2 is the fat-loss lever, not diet restriction alone.** Three sessions per week at 65–75% max HR (nasal breathing sustainable, full sentences possible) develops mitochondrial fat oxidation without interference with strength adaptation. San Millan & Brooks (2018): Zone 2 does not cause the interference effect; Zone 3–4 does.

5. **Volume ramp across phases, not load ramp.** Load increases naturally with adaptation. The program controls volume explicitly: Phase 1 establishes movement quality at 2 sets, Phase 2 adds the third set, Phase 3 adds a myo-rep finisher. All at 2 RIR.

---

## Recomposition Parameters

- **Protein target:** 225g/day (1g/lb bodyweight). Split across 4 meals (~55g each) to hit the leucine threshold (~3g leucine) at each meal and maximize protein synthesis windows.
- **Caloric position:** 200–300 kcal below TDEE. Deeper cuts suppress muscle protein synthesis and blunt training response. The Zone 2 × 3/week + daily walk creates the deficit gap without dietary aggression.
- **Recomposition condition:** Best results when returning to structured training or at higher body fat %. Simultaneous muscle gain and fat loss is real but slower than dedicated bulk/cut phases — the trade-off is sustainability and joint/tendon preservation.

## Weekly Structure

Sunday is the week anchor — the primary session, taken with the most recovery behind it (full Saturday rest). The toggle determines whether Sunday is Lower or Upper Heavy for that week. Monday is the complement — it works different muscle groups, so the split provides the recovery, not a reduced prescription.

```
Sun  — PRIMARY        [toggle: LOWER HEAVY or UPPER HEAVY]   45 min
Mon  — Secondary      [complement to Sunday]                  40 min
Tue  — Zone 2         Outdoor cycling                         45–60 min
Wed  — 3rd strength   [see toggle table]                      40 min
Thu  — 4th strength   [see toggle table]                      40 min
Fri  — Skill Day      Sub-maximal rings                       40 min
Sat  — Zone 2         Treadmill + vest + walk                 45 min
```

### Weekly Toggle

Set once at the start of each week. The choice determines the session order for all four strength days.

| Day | Lower-Heavy Week | Upper-Heavy Week |
|---|---|---|
| Sun | Lower A (heavy — squat/hinge) | Upper A (heavy — push/pull) |
| Mon | Upper A (complement) | Lower A (complement) |
| Wed | Upper B | Lower B |
| Thu | Lower B | Upper B |
| Fri | Skill Day | Skill Day |

**How to choose:** Default alternates week to week. Override when you have a preference — e.g., you know Saturday will involve a long bike ride (lower fatigue carry-over), so choose Upper Heavy to keep legs fresher.

Each muscle group hits 2 true strength sessions per week + 1 sub-maximal skill session (Fri). Total first sets per muscle per week: 4 compound + ongoing GtG exposure on Fri.

## Zone 2 Protocol

**Primary modality (Tue):** Outdoor cycling.
**Secondary modality (Sat):** Treadmill walk + weighted vest (10–15 lb) + walk.

**HR target:** 65–75% max HR. Verified via Polar HR monitor. Rule of thumb: can hold a full sentence without pausing. If you need to pause mid-sentence, drop intensity.

**Duration:** 45 min minimum. 60 min preferred. Below 45 min produces minimal mitochondrial adaptation.

**Why cycling is primary:** Lowest joint stress at 225 lb. Easy to sustain Zone 2 cadence for 45–60 min. Treadmill walk + vest on Saturday because it follows Friday skill day — lower intensity pairs well with a sub-maximal training day and sets up full recovery before Sunday's primary session.

**Tai chi / dancing:** Counts as active recovery, not Zone 2. HR is too variable and uncontrolled. Use freely on rest days or in the morning — does not replace Zone 2 sessions.

## Session Specifications

### UPPER A — Push Emphasis

**Pattern:** Ring push/pull → KB press/row → prehab accessory

| Block | Exercise A1 | Exercise A2 | Pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| Superset A | Ring Push-Up | Ring Row | Push ↔ Pull (horizontal) |
| Superset B | KB Push Press | KB Single-Arm Row | Press ↔ Row (vertical/horizontal) |
| Accessory C | Band External Rotation | — | Myo-reps (rotator cuff) |

**Phase volume:**
- Phase 1: A × 2 rounds, B × 2 rounds, C × 1 activation set + 4 clusters
- Phase 2: A × 3 rounds, B × 3 rounds, C × 1 activation set + 4 clusters
- Phase 3: A × 3 rounds, B × 3 rounds, C myo-reps + loaded stretch hold (ring fly bottom, 30s)

**Rest:** 60s between superset rounds. No rest between A1 and A2 within a round.

**Phase 3 progression:** Add ring fly (A1 swap on alternating weeks) or archer push-up.

---

### UPPER B — Pull Emphasis

**Pattern:** Pull/push → row/fly → core accessory

| Block | Exercise A1 | Exercise A2 | Pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| Superset A | Pull-Up | Ring Dip | Pull ↔ Push (vertical) |
| Superset B | KB Single-Arm Row | Decline Ring Push-Up | Row ↔ Press (horizontal) |
| Accessory C | Dead Bug | — | Core (straight sets, not myo-reps) |

**Phase volume:**
- Phase 1: A × 2, B × 2, C × 2 sets × 10 reps
- Phase 2: A × 3, B × 3, C × 2 sets × 12 reps
- Phase 3: A × 3 (add towel hang on final round), B × 3, C × 2 sets + scapular pull-up × 10

**Rest:** 75s between superset rounds on pull-up/dip (higher CNS demand).

**Phase 3 progression:** Add weight vest to pull-up for top-end rounds. Ring row with feet elevated as regression.

---

### LOWER A — Squat Emphasis

**Pattern:** Single-leg squat / hinge → reactive → ankle/knee prehab

| Block | Exercise A1 | Exercise A2 | Pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| Superset A | KB Bulgarian Split Squat | KB Romanian Deadlift | Squat ↔ Hinge (unilateral) |
| Superset B | Nordic Hamstring Eccentric | Jump Squat | Knee flexion ↔ Explosive extension |
| Ankle block C | Tibialis Raise + Single-Leg Soleus Raise | — | Straight sets (not myo-reps) |

**Phase volume:**
- Phase 1: A × 2, B × 2, C × 1 set each (20 reps tib / 15 reps soleus per side)
- Phase 2: A × 3, B × 2, C × 2 sets each
- Phase 3: A × 3, B × 3, C × 2 sets + pogo hop × 20 (reactive ankle close-out)

**Notes:**
- Nordic: 5 reps, 5-second lower, use hands to return. This is prehab, not a strength set — 2 RIR doesn't apply here.
- Jump squat: 6 reps, full reset each rep. Explosive intent, soft landing. Not myo-reps.
- Ankle block: If morning F4 ankle block already completed, show as "completed" and skip. One owner per session.

**Rest:** 75s between A rounds (bilateral leg work is high systemic demand).

---

### LOWER B — Hinge Emphasis

**Pattern:** Hinge / squat → posterior chain isolation → hip/glute prehab

| Block | Exercise A1 | Exercise A2 | Pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| Superset A | KB Romanian Deadlift | KB Goblet Squat | Hinge ↔ Squat (bilateral) |
| Superset B | Slider Hamstring Curl | KB Suitcase Carry | Knee flexion ↔ Anti-lateral carry |
| Prehab C | Lateral Band Walk + Glute Bridge | — | Hip/glute circuit |

**Phase volume:**
- Phase 1: A × 2, B × 2, C × 1 round (20 steps band walk + 15 bridges)
- Phase 2: A × 3, B × 2, C × 2 rounds
- Phase 3: A × 3, B × 3 (swap carry for KB woodchop in alternating weeks), C × 2 rounds + single-leg bridge × 10/side

**Notes:**
- Suitcase carry: 30m or 40 steps per side. Anti-lateral flexion drill — not cardiovascular.
- Glute bridge in C block: tempo 2-2-2. Not myo-reps.

---

### SKILL DAY — Friday (Sub-Maximal, Zero Recovery Debt)

This is practice, not training. No set is taken close to failure. No rest timer needed.

**Structure (order flexible):**

| Movement | Prescription | Intent |
|---|---|---|
| Pull-Up or Ring Row | 5–6 reps × 4–6 sets (GtG, spread across session) | Neural efficiency, not hypertrophy |
| Jefferson Curl | 5 reps × 2 sets, 5s lower, 5 lb max | Spinal prep, not deadlift |
| Skin the Cat | 3–5 reps × 2 sets (tuck or straight body) | Shoulder ROM skill |
| Active Hang | 30s × 3 | Shoulder decompression |
| Shoulder CARs | 5/side | Joint maintenance |
| Hip CARs | 5/side | Hip capsule maintenance |
| 90/90 Hip Stretch | 60s/side | Hip IR/ER maintenance |
| Pancake stretch or Pigeon Prep | 90s/side | Adductor/hip ER length |

**What to add by phase:**
- Phase 1: Tuck skin the cat only. GtG pull-ups.
- Phase 2: Progress skin the cat to bent-arm → straight-arm. Add Ab Roller × 8 (controlled).
- Phase 3: Add L-sit attempts (paralettes or rings) × 3 × 10s holds. GtG with weighted vest on pull-ups.

**Friday is not a rest day.** It is active skill work. The absence of fatigue is the point.

## Phase Structure

### Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

**Volume:** 2 working sets per superset. 3 RIR first week, 2 RIR weeks 2–4.
**Intent:** Establish movement quality, joint tolerance, and consistent habit before adding load.
**Load rule:** Start at 60–65% of perceived max. Add load only when both sets are comfortably completed at 2 RIR with clean form.

**Why explanations in app:**
> "2 sets only. This is the research-backed minimum effective dose — and it's not a compromise. Your first set delivers the most stimulus; each additional set delivers less. This phase builds the base without accumulating damage that would slow adaptation."

---

### Phase 2 — Volume Build (Weeks 5–8)

**Volume:** 3 working sets per superset. 2 RIR throughout.
**Intent:** Increase total weekly volume by adding the third set. Load continues to progress.
**Load rule:** Add load when all 3 sets are completed at 2 RIR for 2 consecutive sessions.

**Why explanations in app:**
> "Third set added. You have a full week of adaptation behind each movement — the extra set now recovers in time for the next session. Volume doubled from Phase 1, same recovery cost."

---

### Phase 3 — Intensification (Weeks 9–12)

**Volume:** 3 working sets + 1 myo-rep finisher on primary accessories. 2 RIR on compound sets; myo-rep activation set to ~1 RIR.
**Intent:** Push effective rep density on accessories without adding more compound sets. Load is highest this phase.
**Load rule:** Hold load constant. Focus on proximity — closer to 2 RIR means better quality, not more weight.

**Myo-rep protocol:**
1. Activation set: 12–15 reps to ~1 RIR. Rack/stop.
2. Rest 5 seconds.
3. Mini-set: 4–5 reps. Rest 5s. Repeat × 4 clusters.
4. Stop when you cannot complete 3 reps cleanly.

**Why explanations in app:**
> "Myo-reps keep you in the effective rep zone — the last 5 reps before failure — without accumulating the damage of full set restarts. Same stimulus density in half the time."

## GYR Readiness Modifications

The readiness check runs before each strength session. Zone 2 days always proceed regardless of GYR.

| Signal | Modification |
|---|---|
| 🟢 Green | Full session as prescribed. |
| 🟡 Yellow | Reduce each superset by 1 round. Skip myo-rep finisher. Drop KB load by one step (~10%). Keep all movements — no exercise swaps. |
| 🔴 Red | Morning prehab F1–F4 only. Zone 2 walk (not cycling) replacing strength session. No strength work. |

**Red day rule:** A Red result is data, not failure. Two consecutive Red days = mandatory Zone 2 + sleep audit before returning to strength. Check: sleep quality, protein intake, hydration, life stress. Do not push through.

## Night Recovery

Unchanged from current `daily-practice.md`. Physiological Sigh → Supine Thoracic Release → Exhale-Only Supine Reset → Crocodile Breathing → 4-7-8 Breath. 7 movements total.

Sleep architecture is the primary recovery lever. The night protocol exists to lower resting HR and cortisol before sleep onset — not as a workout.

## Deload Protocol (After Each Phase)

One deload week between phases. Deload ≠ rest week.

- Strength sessions: 1 set per superset, 4 RIR (very easy). Keep all movements.
- Zone 2: Full sessions. This is the week Zone 2 does the most work.
- Skill Day: Keep as normal — it's already sub-maximal.
- Morning/night practice: Unchanged.
