# Charlie Francis

## Role In Workout OS

Charlie Francis is useful as the high-low CNS organization influence. Workout OS uses this lens to place hard days, recovery days, skill work, and conditioning so stress does not blur into one exhausting middle zone.

## Source Links

- Charlie Francis Training System reference: https://charliefrancis.com/
- General public overview: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charlie_Francis

## Core Domains

- Sprint training organization.
- High-low training model.
- CNS stress management.
- Separating speed/power days from recovery days.
- Recovery as a planned training input.

## Principles To Extract

1. **Hard days should be truly hard, easy days truly easy**
   - Avoid making every day medium.
   - Workout OS translation: High -> Moderate -> Low -> Moderate/High -> Moderate -> Low -> Low weekly wave.

2. **CNS stress is not the same as muscular soreness**
   - A user can feel motivated while the nervous system is under-recovered.
   - Workout OS translation: readiness includes sleep, RHR/HRV, energy, and motivation.

3. **Low days can accelerate recovery**
   - Zone 2, mobility, easy skill, and tissue work can support adaptation.
   - Workout OS translation: Tuesday and Saturday are recovery-biased by design.

4. **Skill quality drops under fatigue**
   - High-coordination work should not be placed after exhaustive loading unless deliberately simplified.
   - Workout OS translation: skill days are low-CNS, not hidden conditioning tests.

## Workout OS Rules

- Do not place two heavy CNS days back to back.
- Yellow readiness converts hard work to lower-volume pattern work.
- Skill work has intensity caps unless the block is skill-specialized.
- Recovery days must not become junk-volume days.

## Original Example Sequence

**High-low week shape**

```text
Day 1: High — Heavy Push + Support Pull
Day 2: Moderate — Lower pattern work
Day 3: Low — Zone 2 + mobility
Day 4: Moderate/High — Support Push + Heavy Pull
Day 5: Moderate — Lower B or skill strength
Day 6: Low — Mobility / active recovery
Day 7: Low or off — walk, breath, recovery
```

## What Not To Copy

- Do not copy sprint programs for non-sprinters.
- Do not add max-speed sprinting without preparation.
- Do not use "high-low" as an excuse to make low days long and draining.
